Studying in China Without Speaking Chinese: Your Complete Guide to English-Taught Programs

The prospect of studying in China without speaking Mandarin might seem daunting, but the reality is far more encouraging than you might expect. Majority of Chinese universities offer English-taught bachelor's programs, and the options continue to expand each year. Whether you're considering undergraduate or graduate studies, China's universities have developed comprehensive English-medium programs designed specifically for international students who don't speak Chinese.

The Growing Landscape of English-Taught Programs

Thousands of English-taught programs are now available at China's top universities. From Medicine to AI, Business to Engineering, China has positioned itself as a global education hub with fully English-medium courses designed for international students. This transformation reflects China's commitment to internationalization and its recognition of the global demand for accessible higher education.

Tsinghua University currently offers 35 graduate programs in English, while many other top universities have followed suit with extensive English-taught offerings. The number of English-taught Bachelors degrees in the world has increased explosively in the last couple of years. Some of the countries with the most English-taught study programmes include Australia, Belgium, China, Denmark, Germany, Italy, the Netherlands, Spain, Switzerland, United Kingdom and the United States.

Undergraduate Programs in English

Duration and Structure

To complete undergraduate studies in China, you have to dedicate 4 to 6 years of comprehensive learning. Courses in the medical field such as the Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS) and Bachelor of Clinical Medicine are examples of programs that are completed in 6 years. Most other undergraduate programs follow the standard four-year structure common in international education systems.

Available Fields of Study

STEM Fields

  • Mechanical and Manufacturing Engineering
  • Civil Engineering
  • Electrical and Electronic Engineering
  • Computer Science and Technology
  • Chemical Engineering
  • Environmental Engineering

Medical Programs (MBBS)

Only 44 universities in China are qualified to teach the MBBS degrees in English and authorized to admit foreign students, according to the Chinese Ministry of Education (MoE). The MoE has also said teaching MBBS courses under a bilingual curriculum in English and Chinese is "strictly prohibited". This ensures quality control and standardization for international medical students.

Some of the top medical schools in China with the best English-taught MBBS programs include Shantou University Medical College (SUMC), Capital Medical University (CMU), Xiamen University (XMU) School of Medicine, Jiangsu University (JSU) Medical School and Guangzhou Medical University (GMU).

Business and Economics

  • International Economics and Trade

  • Marketing
  • E-commerce

Liberal Arts and Social Sciences

  • Journalism and Media Studies
  • International Relations

  • Teaching Chinese as a Foreign Language

Entry Requirements

If you're attending a degree programme in China, you will need to prove that your language skills are good enough to participate in the classes and understand the lectures. These courses will also prepare you for any of the English-language tests that universities require.

  • High school completion certificate
  • English proficiency proof (TOEFL/IELTS), though IELTS and TOEFL are not required at some universities
  • Academic transcripts
  • Students must have a minimum of 60% marks in Senior High School (HSSC)/WAEC and are eligible to apply for Medical Study in China

Graduate Programs in English

Master's Programs

Tsinghua University has over 30 English-taught master's degrees in subjects like business, law, engineering, and the social sciences. Graduate programs typically last 2-3 years and cover advanced specializations across various fields.

Popular English-taught master's programs include:

  • MBA programs at top business schools
  • Engineering specializations (Robotics, AI, Automation)
  • Public Health and Epidemiology
  • International Relations and Diplomacy
  • Computer Science and Data Science

Doctoral Programs

Many Chinese universities offer PhD programs in English, particularly in STEM fields where research collaboration is international by nature. These programs typically last 3-4 years and involve conducting research under the supervision of professors who often have international experience.

Types of English-Taught Programs

International Student-Only Programs

If you study in this type of English taught program, you won't take any classes with local students. If your main purpose to study in China is building guanxi (relationship) and hopefully this relationship can be helpful for future career. You should not enroll in this type of program. Most of the English taught programs provided by local universities are international student admission only program.

Mixed Programs with Local Students

There are international universities in China that provide programs with international curriculum. Students will receive foreign university academic credits and degrees. International universities in China have their own campuses and have operated in China with excellent reputation for years. They also recruit local Chinese students through the China's National College Entrance Examination of China (or Gaokao). You will take all classes with local students and all courses are taught in English.

Top Universities for English-Taught Programs

Joint Venture Universities

University of Nottingham Ningbo China (UNNC) was the first Sino-foreign university to open its doors in China. Established in 2004, with the full approval of the Chinese Ministry of Education, we are run by the University of Nottingham in partnership with Zhejiang Wanli University, a key player in the education sector in China.

Xian Jiaotong-Liverpool University (XJTLU) is a joint university founded by Xian Jiaotong University and the University of Liverpool. Undergraduate students can receive two degrees: XJTLU degree and UK degree from the University of Liverpool.

Financial Considerations

Tuition Fees

At public universities, tuition ranges between 2,500 and 10,000 USD/year. At private universities, tuition can reach 30,000 USD and sometimes over 50,000 USD/year depending on your chosen discipline.

For medical programs specifically, yearly tuition for MBBS ranges from US $3,200 to $11,000, while The annual cost of Clinical Medicine (MBBS) at MOE-listed medical universities ranges from 35,000 RMB to 40,000 RMB ($5000 to $6000) Per year, including tuition and accommodation expenses. If we calculate in Pakistani rupees (PKR), it is 14 to 15 lakh per year including tuition and accommodation.

Living Costs

China can be a cost-effective destination for international students. Average undergraduate tuition runs between $1,000 to $5,000 USD per semester. Studying abroad in China through a program provider can cost anywhere between $10,000 to $25,000 USD per semester. This price generally includes tuition, housing, cultural activities, support, and more.

Language Support and Requirements

No Chinese Required for Admission

You don't need to be fluent in Chinese language to study in bachelor or master degree program in China. International students can receive education with medium of instruction in English and explore potential opportunities in China for the future.

Q: Do I need to know Chinese to study these programs? A: No. English is the medium of instruction, but basic Chinese is taught for daily living.

Chinese Language Learning Opportunities

  • Free Chinese language courses for international students studying in English taught programs
  • Basic Chinese classes for daily communication
  • HSK preparation courses for those interested in learning the language

Do I need to learn Chinese to study MBBS in China? No, the MBBS programs are taught in English, but learning basic Chinese can be helpful for clinical practice during the internship.

Quality Assurance and Recognition

International Accreditation

All bachelor's degrees conferred by universities in China are accredited and internationally-recognized. Degrees from top Chinese universities are widely recognized by employers and educational institutions globally.

Program Standards

Accreditation is important when you apply for English taught program in China. It helps determine if the program meets or exceeds minimum quality standards. Universities maintain high standards through:

  • International faculty recruitment
  • Use of international textbooks
  • Regular quality assessments
  • Partnerships with foreign universities

Post‚ÄëGraduation Opportunities

Employment Prospects

Q: Can I work after graduation? A: Yes. Many cities like Shanghai and Shenzhen allow foreign graduates to apply for work visas post-study. China's growing economy and international business connections create numerous opportunities for English-speaking graduates.

Further Studies

  • Continue to higher degrees in China or internationally
  • Apply for research positions
  • Pursue professional certifications in their home countries

Practical Considerations

Campus Life

  • International student dormitories
  • English-speaking support staff
  • International student associations
  • Cultural integration programs
  • English signage and documentation

Daily Life Challenges

  • Shopping and dining may require basic Chinese or translation apps
  • Administrative procedures might need assistance
  • Social integration with local students may be limited in some programs

Making the Right Choice

  1. Program Type: Whether you prefer international-only or mixed classes with local students
  2. University Ranking: Both domestic and international rankings
  3. Location: Major cities offer more international amenities but higher costs
  4. Industry Connections: Especially important for professional programs
  5. Support Services: Quality of international student services

Conclusion

Studying in China without speaking Chinese is not only possible but increasingly common and well-supported. With thousands of English-taught programs across all academic levels and disciplines, international students can access quality education at competitive prices while experiencing one of the world's most dynamic economies.

The key to success lies in careful program selection, understanding the different types of English-taught programs available, and being prepared for the cultural experience beyond the classroom. Whether you choose a joint-venture university offering Western-style education or a traditional Chinese university with strong English programs, the opportunity to study in China without Chinese language skills has never been more accessible.

As China continues to invest in internationalization and expand its English-taught offerings, the options for non-Chinese speaking international students will only continue to grow, making China an increasingly attractive destination for global education.
