Chang'an University, a distinguished institution in China, offers a compelling array of programs for international students. This guide provides an overview of the application process, available programs, and scholarship opportunities, drawing from the university's official International Education School website.

All data checked on September 12, 2025.

Diverse Academic Offerings

Chang'an University primarily offers programs taught in Chinese, spanning undergraduate, master's, and doctoral levels. The university's academic breadth is impressive, covering a wide range of disciplines crucial for global development and infrastructure.

Undergraduate Programs: A Glimpse into Specializations

The university's undergraduate programs are meticulously designed to provide students with specialized knowledge and practical skills. Here's a selection of the schools and their key offerings:

  • School of Highway: This school is at the forefront of infrastructure development, offering programs such as Road, Bridge and River-crossing Engineering and City Underground Space Engineering. These programs are vital for students aspiring to contribute to modern transportation networks and urban planning.
  • School of Automobile: For those passionate about the automotive industry, the School of Automobile provides comprehensive programs including Intelligent Vehicle Engineering, Vehicle Engineering, Logistics Engineering, and Automobile Service Engineering. These cater to the evolving demands of smart and sustainable transportation.
  • School of Construction Machinery: Students interested in heavy machinery and manufacturing can explore programs like Mechanical Engineering, Intelligent Manufacturing Engineering, Mechanical Design Manufacture and Automation, and Mechatronic Engineering. These programs emphasize innovation in industrial applications.
  • School of Economics and Management: This school offers a broad spectrum of business and economic studies, including Logistics Management, Business Administration, Marketing, Accounting, Engineering Management, Information Management and Information System, Economic Statistics, and International Economics and Trade. These prepare students for leadership roles in diverse economic sectors.

Beyond these, numerous other schools, such as the School of Electronics and Control Engineering, Information Engineering School, and School of Geological Engineering and Geomatics, contribute to the university's rich academic tapestry, offering specialized programs that align with global industry needs.

Postgraduate Programs: Advancing Your Expertise

For students seeking to deepen their academic or research pursuits, Chang'an University offers master's and doctoral programs. Prospective postgraduate students are encouraged to identify and connect with a supervisor through the university's official channels, a crucial step in the application process for advanced degrees.

Scholarship Opportunities: Funding Your Education

Chang'an University is committed to attracting talented international students by offering various scholarship programs. The Chinese Government Scholarship (University Program) is a prominent option, providing significant support to eligible candidates.

Chinese Government Scholarship (University Program) Overview

This scholarship is a highly sought-after opportunity for international students. Key eligibility criteria and requirements include:

  • Eligibility: Applicants must be non-Chinese nationals in good health. Age restrictions apply: undergraduate applicants must be under 25, master's applicants under 35, and doctoral applicants under 40.
  • Language Proficiency:
    • For Chinese-Medium Programs, applicants need an HSK 4 certificate (for Bachelor's) or HSK 5 certificate (for Master's/Doctoral) with a minimum score of 180.
    • For English-Medium Programs, acceptable scores include TOEFL IBT 68, IELTS 5.5, or DUOLINGO 85 or above. Students from countries where English is the native or official language may be exempt from this requirement.
  • Application Process: The application for this scholarship involves a dual process:
    1. Applicants must first apply through the China Scholarship Council (CSC) website (http://www.csc.edu.cn/studyinchina), using Agency No: 10710 and Program Category: Type B.
    2. Concurrently, an application must be submitted via Chang'an University's admission portal (http://admission.chd.edu.cn).
    3. A non-refundable application fee of RMB 600 is required, payable online through the university's system.
  • Application Deadline: While specific deadlines can vary annually, the information indicates a deadline of March 31, 2022. Prospective applicants should always verify the most current deadlines on the official university website to ensure timely submission.

The Application Journey: Step-by-Step Guide

The application process for Chang'an University is streamlined through an online system, requiring careful attention to detail and documentation.

  1. Online Submission: All applications are exclusively processed through the university's online application system, accessible at http://admission.chd.edu.cn. This centralized platform ensures efficient handling of applications.
  2. Application Fee: A mandatory, non-refundable application fee of RMB 600 is required for all applicants. This fee must be paid online as part of the application submission.
  3. Essential Documentation: Applicants must prepare and upload a comprehensive set of documents. These typically include:
    • The CSC Application Form, specifically for those applying for the Chinese Government Scholarship.
    • The highest diploma obtained, along with a notarized copy. For current students, an On-study Certificate from their university is necessary.
    • Relevant academic transcripts, also requiring notarized copies.
    • A study or research plan, which must be at least 500 words. This plan should be written in Chinese for Chinese-medium programs and in English for English-medium programs.
    • Two letters of recommendation from professors or associate professors, written in either Chinese or English.
    • A completed Foreigner Physical Examination Form, filled out in English, with results valid for six months.
    • For Master's and Doctoral degree programs, a Conditional Acceptance Letter from a Chang'an University supervisor is required. Information on finding a supervisor is available on the university's website.
    • Proof of language proficiency, such as HSK certificates for Chinese-medium programs or TOEFL, IELTS, or DUOLINGO scores for English-medium programs.
    • Any certificates of academic achievements or participation in cultural and sports activities can also be submitted to strengthen the application.

Important Considerations

  • All submitted materials must be in Chinese or English, and certificates and transcripts must be notarized.
  • The application fee is non-refundable, regardless of admission outcome.
  • Applicants should refrain from sending any physical paper materials to the university.
  • Students who apply for the Chinese Government Scholarship (CSC) program but are not selected may be considered for the CAU Xi'an Municipal 'One Belt One Road' Scholarship without needing to reapply or pay an additional fee.
