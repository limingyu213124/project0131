Quick Summary: BLCU's main campus dorms are on the Haidian (Xueyuan Road) campus. Official dorm types are commonly shared rooms (3-, 4-, 6-person), with graduate dorm fees published in a low annual range. The university runs an online bed-selection system, requires signed accommodation agreements at check-in, provides only mattresses in rooms, and arranges certified vendors to sell bedding during move-in. Campus dining, sports facilities and a campus hospital are available nearby.

For more information about studying at BLCU, explore our university selector tool and check our Life in China section for additional living guides.

All information verified as of September 18, 2025.

Key Facts (Official / To Verify on Arrival)

Location and Campus

Main Campus: Haidian District (Xueyuan Road). Several specific dorm buildings are named on the site, including Academic Buildings 7, 8, 17, 18, 19 used as dorm/teaching/meeting complexes.

Room Types and Eligibility

Typical room types listed in official grad-housing notice:

  • 3-person rooms
  • 4-person rooms
  • 6-person rooms

Graduate students are accommodated within the normal study period; part-time or Beijing-domiciled students may not be assigned dorms.

Fee Structure

Graduate Dormitory Fees: 650‚Äì1,020 RMB per academic year depending on room type (official range; undergraduate/international language program rates may differ).

Online Bed Selection System

BLCU uses a "new student accommodation system" with specific procedures:

Selection Process:

  • Simulation Period: Practice using the system before formal selection
  • Formal Selection Period: Choose your preferred bed and room
  • Auto-Assignment: Students who do not pick a bed in time are auto-assigned

Important Steps:

  • After selection, download and sign the accommodation agreement
  • Present the signed agreement at check-in
  • Keep digital copies of all documents

Bedding and Room Setup

What's Provided:

  • Mattress only - rooms are supplied with a mattress only
  • Basic furniture (beds, desks, closets)

What You Need to Buy:

The university contracts certified vendors to sell bedding on move-in day. Students are advised not to buy from unauthorized street vendors.

  • Duvet set and pillow
  • Sheets and mattress cover
  • Towels and basic blankets

Estimated Costs:

Basic good-quality bedding set: 200‚Äì500 RMB if you buy locally (prices vary). Bring at least one towel and a basic blanket if arriving late at night.

Special Accommodations

Extra-Long Beds

If you're tall (>190 cm), apply for an extra-long bed through the provided online form by the stated deadline‚Äîdon't wait until check-in.

Alternative Housing

If you prefer a lower or single-occupancy option, check if the international office or your program can arrange alternative housing (often limited and may charge more).

Check-in Process

Opening Times:

Dorms open at noon the day before official registration; exact times vary by cohort and year‚Äîconfirm your cohort notice.

Documents to Bring:

  • Passport and admission paperwork
  • Signed accommodation agreement (downloaded and signed after online selection)
  • Payment receipts
  • Contact info for your college/international office
  • Medical/insurance paperwork

Campus Services and Facilities

Dining Options:

  • Large multi-canteen building(s) with student/teacher/halal/favored cuisine halls
  • Caf√©s and chain fast-food outlets on/near campus
  • Student canteens are the most economical option

Sports and Recreation:

  • Athletics facilities (track, courts, pool)
  • Sports equipment and recreational areas

Healthcare:

  • On-campus comprehensive hospital with several departments
  • Nearby tertiary hospitals for specialized care
  • Emergency on-campus phone numbers provided in official notices

Other Services:

  • Nearby supermarkets, pharmacies and banks
  • Campus Wi-Fi and IT services
  • Laundry facilities on or off campus

Practical, Evidence-Based Tips (What Many New Students Miss)

Use the Bed-Selection System Proactively

  • Practice during the simulation window and be ready at the formal selection time
  • Auto-assignment often gives less favorable beds (lower floor, farther from bathroom)
  • Download and keep the signed accommodation agreement PDF‚Äîit's required at check-in

Money & Billing

Budget for additional costs beyond dormitory fees:

  • Deposits and bedding costs
  • Campus card top-up
  • Utilities if billed separately
  • Incidental move-in purchases

Keep receipts and payment screenshots for your records.

Health & Safety

  • Save the campus hospital number and local emergency contacts to your phone before arrival
  • Note campus medical resources and emergency procedures
  • Register for campus health services upon arrival

Documentation and Evidence Retention

Save screenshots/emails of your bed selection, accommodation agreement, payment confirmations and any official communications. If a problem arises later (wrong assignment, fee dispute), these are your strongest evidence.

Pre-Arrival Checklist (Actionable)

Essential Preparations:

  • Read the official accommodation notice for your cohort; note all deadlines
  • Register for and test access to the "new student accommodation system" during the simulation window
  • Pack or plan to buy bedding (mattress cover, sheets, duvet, pillow); budget ~200‚Äì500 RMB as a practical estimate
  • Prepare passport, admission letter, printed/PDF accommodation agreement, and payment proofs

Special Requirements:

  • If >190 cm, apply for extra-long bed before the deadline
  • Save International Student Office and campus hospital phone numbers
  • Locate nearest metro/bus stops on arrival

Financial Planning:

  • Plan funds for campus card top-up, local SIM & transport
  • Set aside a small emergency fund (recommendation: 1,000‚Äì2,000 RMB initial)

First Week ‚Äî Practical Actions to Take

Immediate Tasks:

  • Complete check-in and return the signed accommodation agreement to dorm management. Keep a scanned copy
  • Inspect your bed/room: note mattress condition, lights, sockets, window locks and bathroom fixtures; report faults immediately with photo evidence
  • Register for campus Wi-Fi (follow official IT instructions) and top up your campus card for canteen use

Daily Life Setup:

  • Find the nearest laundromat/washer on or off campus, or confirm whether the dorm offers laundry services
  • Introduce yourself to dorm management and ask about quiet hours, visitor rules and bike/motorbike parking rules
  • Explore campus dining options and nearby amenities

If You Need Different/Temporary Housing

Short-term hotels or youth hostels near Xueyuan Road are available but cost more. If you miss dorm selection or need quarantine/temporary stay on arrival, coordinate with the International Student Office for approved short-term arrangements rather than using unverified vendors.
