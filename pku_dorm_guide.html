Peking University offers limited on-campus and near-campus accommodation for international students and short-term program participants. Why This Guide Matters

Accommodation at Peking University is highly sought after and limited in supply. Understanding the booking process, room options, and what to expect will help you secure the best possible housing arrangement for your time at one of China's most prestigious universities.

This guide combines official PKU information with practical insights to help you navigate the accommodation system successfully and prepare for your life on campus in Beijing.

Quick FAQ

Q: How early should I book accommodation?

A: Book immediately when reservations open. Many programs use strict "first come, first served" allocation, and on-campus beds are limited.

Q: What's the difference between hotel-style and student apartments?

A: Hotel-style suites are pricier and more private with daily rates, while student apartments are cheaper and offer more campus life integration.

Q: What if I can't get on-campus accommodation?

A: You'll need to arrange off-campus housing and complete public security registration with local police, plus notify the university of your address.

Q: Are linens and towels provided?

A: Hotel-style rooms often include more furnished items like linens and towels, while standard student dorms may require you to bring your own.

Q: How reliable is the internet in Global Village?

A: Campus Wi-Fi is usually available, but speeds and connectivity rules may vary by building. Confirm bandwidth availability if you have heavy usage needs.

Q: Can I change my room after booking?

A: Room changes are subject to availability and may incur additional fees. Contact the accommodation office for specific policies.

Final Recommendations

Living at Peking University offers an exceptional opportunity to experience campus life at one of China's most prestigious institutions. While accommodation is limited and competitive, proper planning and early booking can secure you a comfortable place to live during your studies.

Keep in touch with the International Students Division for the most up-to-date information, and don't hesitate to reach out to program coordinators with specific questions about your accommodation needs.
