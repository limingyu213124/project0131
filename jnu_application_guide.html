About Jinan University

Founded in 1906, Jinan University is one of China's oldest and most prestigious universities, with a rich history of serving overseas Chinese communities. The university is located in Guangzhou, a major economic hub in southern China, offering students access to excellent career opportunities and cultural experiences.

JNU is renowned for its comprehensive academic programs, strong research capabilities, and international outlook. The university has established partnerships with over 200 universities worldwide, creating a truly global learning environment. Eligibility Requirements

Before diving into the application process, let's check the basic eligibility requirements for international students at JNU:

Basic Requirements

  • Non-Chinese citizen with a valid passport
  • Good health and no criminal record
  • Respectful of Chinese laws and university regulations
  • Genuine interest in learning with a solid academic record

Academic Background Requirements

Bachelor's Programs

High school diploma or equivalent qualification

Master's Programs

Bachelor's degree from an accredited institution

Doctoral Programs

Master's degree from an accredited institution

Language Requirements

JNU offers programs taught in both Chinese and English. Your language proficiency requirement will depend on the language of instruction for your chosen program.

Chinese-Taught Programs

For Chinese-taught programs, you'll need to provide an HSK (Hanyu Shuiping Kaoshi) certificate:

  • Liberal Arts, Economics, Management: HSK 5 (180 points or above)
  • Science, Engineering, Medicine: HSK 4 (180 points or above)

English-Taught Programs

For English-taught programs, non-native English speakers need to demonstrate proficiency through:

  • TOEFL: 80 or above
  • IELTS: 6.0 or above

Pro Tip: If your Chinese isn't quite at the required level, don't worry! JNU's College of Chinese Language and Culture offers excellent preparatory (pre-university) courses to get you up to speed.

Application Process: Step-by-Step Guide

The application process is straightforward if you follow these steps. Bookmark the Jinan University International Student Admission System ‚Äì this will be your best friend throughout the process.

Step 1: Choose Your Program

JNU has a vast array of programs. Spend some time exploring the Undergraduate and Graduate program lists on the official admissions website. Find the one that sparks your interest and check its specific requirements.

Step 2: Prepare Your Documents

This is the most crucial part. Get all your documents scanned and ready for upload. Here's a comprehensive checklist:

Required Documents

  • Online Application Form: Filled out in the JNU admission system
  • Passport: Clear copy of your main information page
  • Highest Diploma/Degree Certificate: Notarized translation if not in Chinese or English
  • Academic Transcripts: Translated and notarized if needed
  • Language Proficiency Certificate: HSK or TOEFL/IELTS report
  • Personal Statement/Study Plan: Around 800 words about your background and goals
  • Two Recommendation Letters: Required for Master's and Doctoral applicants
  • Physical Examination Form: Standard form from embassy or JNU website
  • Blood Test Report: As part of the health examination
  • Certificate of No Criminal Record: Official document issued within 6 months
  • Application Fee Receipt: Non-refundable application fee

Step 3: Apply Online & Pay the Fee

Head over to the JNU International Student Admission System, create your account, fill in all the details accurately, and upload your prepared documents. You'll then be prompted to pay the application fee.

Step 4: Submission and Waiting

Once you've submitted everything, the admissions committee will review your application. You can check your application status through the online portal. If your application is successful, you will receive the official Admission Letter and the JW201/202 Form (for your visa application).

Application Deadlines

Timing is everything. Deadlines can vary slightly each year, so always check the official website for the most current dates. Here's a general timeline:

Autumn Intake (September)

Applications usually open in early spring (March) and close around late June

Spring Intake (February)

Mainly for non-degree language programs. Applications typically run from October to December of the previous year

Important: Scholarship applications often have earlier deadlines than the general university application. Plan ahead and prepare your documents well in advance!

Scholarships and Funding

A university education is an investment, but there's help available. JNU is covered by several major scholarships for international students:

Chinese Government Scholarship (CSC)

The most prestigious scholarship, often covering tuition, accommodation, and a living stipend. Apply through the Chinese Embassy in your home country (Type A) or through the university itself (Type B).

Guangdong Government Outstanding International Student Scholarship

A specific scholarship for students studying in Guangdong province, offering financial support for excellent international students.

Jinan University President's Scholarship

The university's own scholarship to attract excellent talent, providing financial assistance to outstanding international students.
