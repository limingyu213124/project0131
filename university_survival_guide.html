Quick Answer

If you study at a mainland Chinese university, expect classes organized in 45-minute "learning hours" (often delivered as 90-minute blocks), a mix of large lectures, small seminars/tutorials and longer lab/practical sessions, and assessments driven by midterms, final exams and coursework. In mainland China, a "learning hour" commonly equals 45 minutes. 16 learning hours typically count as one credit in many universities, so courses are scheduled in chunks that add up to the credit total listed in the programme guide.

Typical Block Format

Universities often combine two learning hours into a 90‚Äëminute teaching block (or two 45‚Äëminute slots with a short break). Labs, studios or workshops may run for multiple continuous hours to allow setup and hands‚Äëon work.

7. Academic Integrity and Exam Rules (Don't Gamble Here)

Mainland universities enforce strict rules on cheating and fabricated data; consequences include course failure, disciplinary action and, in extreme cases, loss of student status. Read the student management/examination regulations for your school and treat them as binding.

For writing: always cite, and when paraphrasing, change wording and structure and still credit the source. Use the writing centre and anti‚Äëplagiarism tutorials many universities provide.
