China offers a wide range of nursing programs that suit different goals. Whether you want an English-taught bachelor's degree, clinical training in major teaching hospitals, or access to strong research groups, China has options. This comprehensive guide collects the most useful practical details and a curated list of institutions to help you plan your nursing education in China.

All data checked on September 11, 2025.

Why Study Nursing in China?

  • Large clinical systems and teaching hospitals give students broad clinical exposure. The global nursing workforce is growing, but regional gaps remain, which makes well-designed nursing education valuable for graduates seeking international or local careers.
  • English-taught programs available at several Chinese universities with clear international admissions processes, which helps non-Chinese speakers apply and enroll.
  • Affordable costs compared with Western countries. Tuition and living costs in many Chinese programs can be more affordable, and some universities offer scholarships for international students.

For more information about studying in China, check out our comprehensive Study in China Guide and learn about student visa requirements.

Types of Nursing Programs You'll Find

  • Bachelor of Nursing (4 years) in Chinese or English. English options are available at some universities and are often listed in international admissions brochures.
  • Master and PhD programs for students who want research or academic careers.
  • Short courses and continuing education focused on clinical skills, simulation, and specialization.

Application Essentials and Timelines

  • Check university websites - Visit the international education or nursing school page on each university for application deadlines and required documents. International admission guides often list the precise steps and documents, including passport, transcripts, language certificates, and health checks.
  • Language requirements vary - English-taught programs may ask for English proficiency or interview. Chinese-taught programs commonly require HSK. Confirm requirements early and get certified translations where needed.
  • Reserve time for processing - Allow time for visa processing, medical exam forms for foreign students, and arrival instructions that universities publish for new students.

Tuition, Scholarships, and Living Costs

  • Tuition varies by university and program - For example, one documented English nursing bachelor listing shows tuition in the range provided in the university brochure for international undergraduates.
  • Look for scholarships - Chinese government scholarships, university scholarships, and merit awards are available. International student offices publish scholarship application windows and rules. Check our CSC scholarship guide for more details.

Clinical Training and Licensing

  • Clinical rotations emphasized - Nursing education in China emphasizes clinical rotations in affiliated hospitals. Universities with large affiliated teaching hospitals offer more varied clinical placements. Confirm clinical hour requirements for program completion on the official program page.
  • International recognition - If you plan to practice outside China, verify whether your target country recognizes the Chinese degree or requires additional exams. Keep copies of clinical practice records and evaluation letters.

Application Checklist

Here's a practical checklist you can use for your application:

  • Passport copy and valid travel documents
  • Official transcripts and diploma copies with translations if required
  • Language proof: IELTS/TOEFL or HSK as specified
  • Medical examination for foreigners as required by the university or visa authority
  • Two recommendation letters if applying for postgraduate programs
  • Application fee payment receipt and completed online application form

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Are English-taught nursing programs available in China?

A: Yes. Several universities publish English program options or list nursing among international undergraduate offerings. Always confirm the language and course list on the official international admissions page of the university you target.

Q: How long is a Bachelor of Nursing program?

A: Typically four years for undergraduate nursing programs in China.

Q: Will a Chinese nursing degree allow me to work elsewhere?

A: Recognition depends on the destination country. Some countries require additional exams or bridging steps. Keep clinical records and diploma documentation for credential evaluation.

Q: What are the language requirements for nursing programs?

A: English-taught programs typically require IELTS or TOEFL scores, while Chinese-taught programs require HSK certification. Requirements vary by university, so check each program's specific requirements.
